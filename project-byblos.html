This project was developed as part of our Final Year Project at the American University of Beirut, my team and I aimed to digitally preserve the Byblos Citadel, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. We began by visiting the citadel and taking thousand of photographs from multiple angles using professional and smartphone cameras. These images were processed using Agisoft Metashape to generate precise 3D photogrammetry models. We then refined and cleaned up the models using Blender, focusing on accuracy, texture clarity, and architectural detail.

Once the models were finalized, we rendered key views and designed a dedicated, interactive website that presents the citadel in a virtual experience. I was also responsible for designing the website in Figma and developing it using responsive HTML, CSS, and JavaScript to ensure accessibility and engagement for a wide audience.

You can check out the 3d models on our website or Sketchfab.

Our team recently participated in an international event held at AUB Mediterraneano in Cyprus, focused on the digitalization of the Byblos Citadel. The event brought together students from 11 universities across the MENA region and Germany, with a total of 25 participating teams. Each team showcased their innovative projects through poster presentations, which were reviewed by attending professors.

We are proud to share that our team was selected as one of three winners, receiving the award for Best Poster and Presentation among all competing teams.
